Causes of Fires

1. Human Causes

  • Cooking accidents: Leaving stoves, ovens, or grills unattended.
  • Smoking: Carelessly discarding lit cigarettes or matches.
  • Electrical faults:
    • Overloaded power outlets.
    • Faulty or frayed wires.
    • Short circuits in appliances.
  • Negligence: Leaving candles burning, improper use of heaters or fireworks.
  • Arson: Intentionally setting fires.

2. Natural Causes

  • Lightning strikes igniting trees or grass.
  • Volcanic activity: Lava can start wildfires.
  • Spontaneous combustion: Hay, compost, or oily rags catching fire.
  • Wildfires during droughts: Dry vegetation easily ignites.

3. Environmental and Weather Factors

  • High temperatures drying plants and wood.
  • Strong winds spreading flames quickly.
  • Low humidity making air dry.
  • Accumulated flammable materials: Dry leaves, paper, trash, or dry grass.

4. Industrial and Chemical Causes

  • Chemical reactions: Mixing flammable chemicals incorrectly.
  • Sparks from machinery: Welding, grinding, or metal cutting.
  • Gas leaks: Propane, natural gas, or fuel.
  • Flammable liquids: Gasoline, alcohol, paints.

5. Miscellaneous Causes

  • Electrical storms creating sparks in power lines.
  • Friction: Some tools create sparks.
  • Explosions from gas tanks or compressed air tanks.

Key Point: The Fire Triangle

Fires need three things to start and grow:

  • Fuel: Something to burn.
  • Heat: Enough to ignite the fuel.
  • Oxygen: Air to keep it burning.

Remove one of these, and the fire will stop!
